Keripik Sanjai Balado.

A must-have Indonesian Snack.

Keripik Sanjai Balado is one of my all-time favorite, elusive snacks. Originating from West Sumatra, Indonesia, they are Cassava Chips that are thinly sliced and coated in Balado spices. Known as Bumbu Balado in Indonesia, the spices are made from sugar and red chili pepper paste.

This is an amazing snack that tastes like nothing I've ever personally tried as a Canadian. I cannot find it in any stores nearby, nor can I find it online. I would like to spread the word of this amazing Indonesian snack. It's an injustice that they're so rare in the west, and more people should know about them! They are crispy chips coated in Balado, a flavor/seasoning that is like nothing else I know about. A bit of spice to them, just right! You owe it to yourself to try this at least once.

It's a crime against humanity to keep this from the west!

Ingredients

  • 5 Red Onions. Coarsely Mashed.
  • 5 Large Red Chilies. Coarsely Mashed.
  • 5 Curly Red Chilies. Coarsely Mashed.
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 50g Brown Sugar.
  • 1 tbsp Sugar
  • 1½ tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Tamarind with 2 tbsp of water.
  • 3 tbsp oil

Let's make some Balado!

  1. Heat your oil.
  2. Add the mashed onions, chilies and bay leaves.
  3. Stir-fry until fragrant.
  4. Add your salt, brown sugar, white sugar and tamarind water.
  5. Stir until the seasoning thickens.

Where the magic happens.

  1. Put your seasoning in a bowl.
  2. Add your Cassava Chips.
  3. Mix gently
  4. Remove the chips and repeat process until finished.
  5. Let dry and enjoy!

FIN

I hope you enjoy this fantastic snack from Indonesia! I hope one day it becomes popularized from the west, so I can enjoy it more often! A true hidden treasure. Thanks to my wonderful wife for showing me this fantastic treat!
